About BSP

The Bahujan Samaj Party stands for the social transformation and economic emancipation of the Bahujan Samaj, which comprises the Scheduled Castes (SC), Scheduled Tribes (ST), and Other Backward Castes (OBC). Inspired by the philosophy of Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, we work towards creating an egalitarian society.

Our Mission

To achieve social transformation and economic emancipation of the Bahujan Samaj through democratic means and constitutional methods.

Our Values

Equality, justice, brotherhood, and the constitutional principles laid down by Dr. B.R. Ambedkar guide our path forward.

Our Struggle

We continue the struggle for dignity, self-respect, and equal opportunities for all marginalized communities in India.

Vision & Leadership

Our vision is to build a society where every individual has equal opportunities regardless of their caste, creed, or background. We are committed to the ideals of Dr. B.R. Ambedkar and work towards implementing his vision of a just and equitable society.

Party Foundation

April 14, 1984 Foundation

BSP was founded by Kanshi Ram on April 14, 1984, on the birth anniversary of Dr. B.R. Ambedkar. This marked the beginning of organized political representation for the Bahujan Samaj, establishing a platform for the socially and economically marginalized communities to participate in democratic governance.

The party was established with the vision of creating a society based on the principles of Liberty, Equality, Fraternity, and Justice as envisioned by Dr. Ambedkar. The foundation day was strategically chosen to honor the legacy of the chief architect of the Indian Constitution.

First Government Formation

June 1993 Political Victory

BSP formed government in Uttar Pradesh for the first time under Mayawati's leadership, proving that the Bahujan Samaj could achieve political power through democratic means. This historic achievement demonstrated the party's growing influence and the trust placed by the people in its ideology and leadership.

The government implemented several welfare schemes for the marginalized communities and worked towards the inclusive development of all sections of society. This period marked the beginning of BSP's political ascendancy in Indian politics.

Historic Absolute Majority

May 2007 Landmark Victory

BSP won absolute majority in UP Assembly elections, forming government with 206 seats out of 403. This was a landmark achievement showing the party's growing influence across all sections of society and its ability to build a coalition that transcended traditional caste boundaries.

The government focused on implementing inclusive governance, infrastructure development, and social welfare programs. This period is remembered as one of the most significant chapters in BSP's political history, demonstrating the power of Ambedkarite ideology.

Strategic Alliance Building

January 2019 Coalition Politics

Formed strategic alliance with Samajwadi Party for Lok Sabha elections, demonstrating commitment to unity among marginalized communities for political empowerment. This alliance showcased the party's pragmatic approach to building broader coalitions for social justice and inclusive development.

The alliance represented a new phase in Indian politics where ideologically aligned parties came together to challenge the dominant political forces and work towards the empowerment of the oppressed sections of society.

December 14, 2024 Celebration

Ambedkar Jayanti Grand Celebration

Celebrating the Legacy of Dr. B.R. Ambedkar

Join us for the grand celebration of Dr. B.R. Ambedkar's birth anniversary, a momentous occasion that honors the architect of the Indian Constitution and the champion of social justice. This year's celebration will be held across all states with unprecedented scale and participation.

Event Highlights

The celebration will feature cultural programs showcasing the rich heritage of the Bahujan community, educational seminars on Dr. Ambedkar's contributions to modern India, and rallies promoting social equality and justice. Distinguished speakers will address the gathering, sharing insights into Dr. Ambedkar's vision for a just and equitable society.

Special tribute programs will be organized at Chaitya Bhoomi, Dr. Ambedkar's final resting place, where thousands of followers pay their respects annually. The event will also include book exhibitions, documentary screenings, and interactive sessions for youth to understand and embrace Ambedkarite ideology.

Participation Details

All party members, supporters, and well-wishers are invited to participate in this grand celebration. State-wise coordination committees have been formed to ensure smooth organization of events. For more information about local celebrations in your area, please contact your nearest BSP office.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about BSP and our movement

What is the main ideology of BSP?

BSP follows the ideology of Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, focusing on social transformation and economic emancipation of the Bahujan Samaj (SC, ST, OBC). We believe in creating an egalitarian society based on principles of Liberty, Equality, Fraternity, and Justice.

How can I become a member of BSP?

You can become a BSP member by contacting your local BSP office or through our official website. Membership is open to all who believe in our ideology and are committed to the cause of social justice and equality.

What are BSP's key policies?

Our key policies include: reservation in government jobs and education for SC/ST/OBC, land reforms, economic empowerment programs, educational advancement, healthcare access, and implementation of constitutional provisions for marginalized communities.

Who founded BSP and when?

BSP was founded by Kanshi Ram on April 14, 1984 (Dr. Ambedkar's birth anniversary). The party was established to provide political representation to the Bahujan Samaj and implement Dr. Ambedkar's vision of social justice.

What is the party symbol and what does it represent?

The BSP symbol is the elephant, which represents strength, wisdom, and memory. The elephant symbolizes the power of the Bahujan Samaj when united and organized under a common political platform.

How does BSP work for women's empowerment?

BSP strongly advocates for women's rights and empowerment. We support reservation for women in legislatures, equal opportunities in education and employment, and protection against discrimination and violence. Our leadership includes strong women leaders like Mayawati.
